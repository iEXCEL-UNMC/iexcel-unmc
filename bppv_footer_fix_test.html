Welcome to the interactive module for the assessment
and treatment of BPPV.

Inside this app, you will be allowed to perform the Dix-Hallpike maneuver to assess for BPPV and observe the resultant nystagmus pattern in order to determine and select the involved semi-circular canal (SCC).

After you’ve correctly identified the canal involved in the BPPV diagnosis, you will enter the treatment mode and perform the appropriate positioning maneuvers to move the displaced otoconia from the SCC back to the utricle.

You will be allowed to reset the position within each scenario as needed if you make an error. You may also randomize the position of the otoconia in order to start the process again and test your knowledge in all 6 SCCs.

Instructions

Click on a category to expand the instructions.

+ Patient Controls
Rotation Controls
The three patient controls in the Patient Controls panel help you rotate the patient's head in all three axes (neck flexion, lateral bending and neck rotation). Use the left and right button on the respective control to rotate the patient's head in that direction. The knob on the blue round slider will indicate the degree of rotation.

Positions
Using the different buttons under the patient rotation controls, you can put the patient in different positions like Laying Down, Prone, Roll Left etc. The ear canals also rotate to reflect the orientation of canals in that position.

You can only go to certain positions from a given position. For example, while the patient is sitting down, you can lay him down, but cannot roll him into prone position. The buttons for positions that you are not allowed to go into from the current positions will be disabled (greyed out). Also, you cannot go into a different position until the current animation completes.

+ Randomize Position
Puts the otoconia in a randomly selected canal and resets the module. You can then start over with diagnosis and treatment maneuvers for a different canal.

+ Reset Position
Puts the otoconia back in the starting position. You can use this in case you make a mistake during the maneuver and want to start over with the diagnosis/treatment of the current scenario.

+ Diagnosis and Treatment Modes
Diagnosis Mode
The application starts in the Diagnosis Mode. Once you perform the diagnosis maneuver, the nystagmus video shows up. Based on the video, once you select the correct canal that otoconia is in and 'Submit Response', the otoconia becomes visible, and you can click 'Proceed to treating the Patient' button to go to Treatment Mode.

Treatment Mode
In the Treatment Mode, You can use the Patient Controls to perform the treatment maneuver and get the otoconia where it should be. A zoomed-in view of the current canal being treated will also be displayed. Once the otoconia reaches the correct region of the ear, a notification mentioning a successful maneuver is displayed, and the otoconia disappears.

You can then exit the application or click 'Restart Module' to place the otoconia in a random canal and reset the module to test your knowledge of a different semi-circular canal.

+ Navigating the 3D Space
You can move around the 3D space in both the main panel, and also in the zoomed-in view in the Treatment Mode, to observe the otoconia movement from different sides/angles.

+ Dark Mode
The button with the moon icon on the top right toggles the User Interface between Dark and Light modes.
